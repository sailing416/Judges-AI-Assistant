Judges Assistant – Under the Racing Rules

Description:
Judges Assistant – Under the Racing Rules is an interactive AI-powered web resource designed to support judges in the sport of sailing across all levels—from club to international. The app focuses on ensuring that the correct procedural steps are followed accurately and consistently, allowing judges to concentrate on applying the Racing Rules of Sailing on the water rather than getting caught up in process details.

The assistant is structured around the core processes that govern a judge’s duties under the Racing Rules of Sailing and related documents. Its value lies in guiding users through each stage of judging—from protest hearings to appeals and requests for redress—while maintaining compliance with official standards and terminology.

Core Features:

Protest Hearings: Step-by-step guidance from validation through to the written decision, including specific criteria for decision-making and a procedural checklist.

Appeals: Complete process support from initial validation to written decision, with embedded criteria for fair and consistent appeal judgments.

Requests for Redress: Detailed process flow from initial request to final written decision, including decision criteria and procedural checklist.

Decision Writing Assistant: Generates judge’s written decisions and automatically produces a second version with suggested standard wording for clarity and consistency.

Reference and Criteria Sections: Includes concise explanations of when and how to apply appeals or requests for redress, ensuring procedural accuracy.

Integration and Access: Hosted via a GitHub link with instructions on obtaining an API key. Users can review information about API access, question-related costs, and where processing fees are directed (note: no fees go to the author).

All content within the app adheres strictly to the official Racing Rules of Sailing documents and associated materials—no alternative terminology is introduced to preserve clarity and avoid ambiguity. The language and structure are designed for a college-level audience, supporting both new and experienced judges in maintaining consistency and confidence in their decisions.
